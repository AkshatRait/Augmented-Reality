Frequently Asked Questions

Augmented Reality (AR) is use of technology to add virtual elements, such as objects, text, and audio, into the real life environment. The combination of virtual elements and the real world provides an enhanced experience of the existing reality.

An example of Augmented Reality is Pokémon GO, which is a game developed by Niantic. Players move around in the real world and catch Pokémon that appears on their mobile device.

Another example is the IKEA Place app. With this app, you can place virtual IKEA furniture into your own space. This way, you can visualize how each furniture looks before buying them.

Virtual Reality is the use of technology to fully immerse the user into a virtual environment. This allows for the user to full experience another reality apart from the real world environment. This differs to Augmented Reality which blends together virtual elements and the real world.

There are many pros and cons that come with Augmented Reality. Some pros may include improving productivity in many industries and help with training different skills. Some cons may include privacy, addiction, and AR being costly.
